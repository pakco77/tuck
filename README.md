## What is tuck

tuck is a minimalist window splitter that lives in your macOS menu bar.

Hold the trigger key (default `Ctrl`) and split layouts appear on screen. Move your mouse to pick a zone, release — the focused window snaps into place. No key combos to memorize, no fiddly config.

> 👉 See the **live demo** right on the homepage: [tuck.pakcochan.com](https://tuck.pakcochan.com)

## Why tuck

- **20+ split layouts** — Full screen / 2 / 3 / 4 panes and many variants (left-right, three columns, quad grid, narrow-wide…). Press `←` `→` to switch layouts, `↑` `↓` to switch variants.
- **Customizable trigger key** — Hold `Ctrl` by default; remap it in the menu if it clashes with something.
- **Multi-display aware** — Each screen remembers its own layout; external and built-in displays stay independent.
- **One-key full screen** — `Ctrl + Space` maximizes the focused window instantly.
- **Swap on occupied** — Drop a window onto a zone that's already taken, and the two windows trade places.

## Install

1. Download `tuck.dmg` from [Releases](https://github.com/pakco77/tuck/releases/latest)
2. Open the dmg and drag **tuck** into Applications
3. Launch tuck (Apple-notarized — Gatekeeper won't block it)

### ⚠️ First launch needs two permissions

tuck has to move windows and to sense your trigger key, so it needs:

| Permission | Path | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Accessibility** | System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility | Lets tuck move / resize windows |
| **Input Monitoring** | System Settings → Privacy & Security → Input Monitoring | Lets tuck detect your trigger key |

Toggle tuck on in both. **Without them, pressing Ctrl does nothing — that's not a bug.**

## Pricing

The core is **free forever**: Full screen / 2 / 3 / 4 panes and all their variants.

Denser layouts (5-pane, 6-pane and beyond) are an optional one-time Pro unlock — yours to keep.

## Privacy

tuck runs entirely on your Mac. It does not collect, transmit, or store any of your data, files, or window contents. Your trigger-key preference and per-display layouts live only on your own device.
